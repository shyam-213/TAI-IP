Magaz

Ingredients


  • Melon Seeds
  • Sugar
  • Almonds
  • Cashews
  • Ghee
  • Cardamom Powder

Optional Ingredients


  • Saffron
  • Pistachios
  • Food Coloring

Instructions


  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, and saut√© until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  5. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until melted and well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss until it's evenly coated with the creamy mushroom sauce.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped fresh parsley.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy your creamy mushroom pasta!

Gulam Jamun

Ingredients


  • Khoya (Mawa)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Rose Water
  • Maida(All-purpose Flour)
  • Ghee or Oil

Optional Ingredients


  • Pistachios or Almonds
  • Condensed Milk

Instructions


  1. Prepare Sugar Syrup:

    In a saucepan, add sugar and water. Bring to a boil over medium heat,
    stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves completely.
    Add crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands (if using).
    Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until it slightly thickens.
    Turn off the heat and add rose water or rose essence (if using).
    Keep the sugar syrup warm.


  2. Prepare Gulab Jamun Dough:

    In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya (mawa) using your fingers
    until it's soft and smooth.Add all-purpose flour (maida) and baking
    powder to the crumbled khoya Mix well. Gradually add milk,
    a little at a time, and knead the mixture to form a smooth,
    soft dough.Be careful not to over-knead.Cover the dough with
    a damp cloth and let it rest for about 10-15 minutes.


  3. Shape and Fry Gulab Jamun:

    Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan or kadhai over medium-low heat.
    Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth,
    crack-free balls. Ensure there are no cracks on the surface, as
    they may break while frying. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide
    the gulab jamun balls into the oil, one by one. Do not overcrowd
    the pan. Fry the gulab jamuns on low to medium heat, stirring
    gently and occasionally, until they turn golden brown and
    evenly cooked from all sides. This may take about 8-10 minutes.
    Remove the fried gulab jamuns using a slotted spoon brand drain
    excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.


  4. Soak in Sugar Syrup:

    While the gulab jamuns are still warm, carefully drop them into
    the warm sugar syrup.Let the gulab jamuns soak in the sugar syrup for at least 1-2 hours or until they become soft, spongy, and well-soaked.
